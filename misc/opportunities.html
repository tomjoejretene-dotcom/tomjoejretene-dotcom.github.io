Technology Opportunities

Both people and society can benefit greatly from robotic prosthesis. These gadgets provide amputees with the potential to regain their independence, physical capabilities, and sense of self-worth (Biddiss & Chau, 2007). Prosthetics are now lighter, more responsive, and able to learn in real time from the user's muscle or nerve impulses thanks to developments in material engineering, artificial intelligence algorithms, and neural connectivity (Clites et al., 2018). In addition to improving quality of life, these improvements lower long-term medical expenses related to dependency and immobility (Hochberg et al., 2012).
