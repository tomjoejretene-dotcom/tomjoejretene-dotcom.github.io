Artificial Body Parts: The Importance of Robotic Prosthetics

Introduction

Bionics is a revolutionary discipline that has emerged recently because of the convergence of robotics, artificial intelligence (AI), and biomedical engineering. The creation and integration of artificial systems that mimic human biological processes is the main goal of bionics (Herr, 2014). Robotic prostheses have been a significant advancement in this sector, enabling people who have lost limbs to regain functionality and mobility. These prosthetic devices detect user intent and provide natural movement using sophisticated sensors, actuators, and neural interfaces (Resnik, Klinger, & Etter, 2019).
